# Research File 01: Primary Speeches & Talks

## Source Type: First-hand (Ilkka Paananen direct output)

### GDC Talk: "The Cell Structure: How Supercell Turned the Traditional Org-Chart Upside Down"
- Source: GDC Vault
- URL: https://www.gdcvault.com/play/1025004/The-Cell-Structure-How-Supercell
- Key points:
  - Supercell's cell structure places power in the hands of development teams, not management
  - Previous experience at Digital Chocolate followed traditional top-down management
  - "The less the management had to do with the game, the better chances of success"
  - Teams of 10-17 developers organized around specific games
  - No mandatory greenlight process from management
  - Teams make all creative decisions autonomously

### Slush 2024 Fireside Chat: "Game On: Getting Uncomfortable to Reignite Growth"
- Source: PocketGamer.biz (full transcript)
- URL: https://www.pocketgamer.biz/full-slush-2024-transcript-supercell-ceo-ilkka-paananen-on-the-clash-of-clans-developers-evolving-culture/
- Key quotes:
  - "We founded Supercell to create these games that as many people as possible would play for years and games that would be remembered forever."
  - "I oftentimes have called myself the least powerful CEO...the more decisions the team makes the better."
  - "Culture is an input, not an output."
  - "Success can be a dangerous thing because oftentimes you start to look to the past."
  - "If that means that we have to go to the uncomfortable zone, then so be it."
  - "Hire people who are smarter and better than you and then trust those people 100%."
  - "We had put our own interests ahead of our players' interest."
  - "As our games need to get better every single week, so does our culture."

### BAFTA Games Lecture (2018)
- Source: PocketGamer.biz / BAFTA
- URL: https://www.pocketgamer.biz/feature/63901/5-lessons-from-ilkka-paananens-bafta-games-lecture/
- Key quotes:
  - "My goal at Supercell is that I would like to be the least powerful CEO"
  - "Focus really is not what you do. It is all the things that you say no to"
  - "We always try to shoot for the moon, well knowing that most likely it's going to fail, but for us that's completely ok."
  - "Games is a form of art. It's not a science"
  - "We don't tolerate average at all"
- Smash Land case study: team deliberated in sauna, asked "If we could develop one game for the next years, is this it?" answered no, killed it themselves
