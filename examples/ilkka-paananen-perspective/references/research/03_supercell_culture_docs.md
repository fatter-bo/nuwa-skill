# Research File 03: Supercell Official Culture Documents & Blog Posts

## Source Type: Supercell official communications

### Supercell Blog: "10 Learnings from 10 Years" (2020)
- URL: https://supercell.com/en/news/10-learnings-10-years/7436/
- Written by Ilkka Paananen
- 10 key learnings:
  1. Always Play the Infinite Game - "Retention, retention, retention. Not just early retention, but always focus on the long term"
  2. Great Teams Make Great Games - Start with "magical pairs" of two people with complete trust
  3. Hire Slowly and Always Raise the Bar - Only hire candidates who increase average quality
  4. Stay as Small as Possible - Small teams avoid "invented work", premature growth increases complexity
  5. Culture Is Actions, Not Slogans - "What you do is who you are"
  6. Replace Control with Trust - "When you remove approvals, processes, hierarchies, and bureaucracies, our people can deliver incredible achievements"
  7. Don't Let Fear Guide You - "No one knows anything! Players are changing, the company is changing, we are changing"
  8. Resist Creating Processes After Mistakes - The "three-month playable rule" caused developers to game the system
  9. Traditional Goal-Setting Doesn't Work - Single overarching goal: "create great games people play for years"
  10. Write Down and Revisit Values - Ilkka's biggest mistake was waiting two years to document culture

### Key Culture Principles (synthesized from multiple sources):
- Independence AND Responsibility (two sides of same coin)
- Teams are accountable to everyone at Supercell, not just CEO
- Daily KPI emails shared company-wide (radical transparency)
- Weekly CEO email to all staff
- Friday all-hands meetings with failure retrospectives
- Champagne toast for killed projects (celebrate learnings, not failure itself)
- No milestone meetings requiring financial justification
- "Gameplay First" prioritization in early development
