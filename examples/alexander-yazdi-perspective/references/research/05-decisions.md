# Alexandre Yazdi - Key Decisions & Strategic Inflection Points

> Research date: 2026-04-12
> Sources: Cross-referenced from interviews, press coverage, financial reporting, industry analysis

---

## 1. The Pivot to Lean Game Development (2015-2016)

### Context
- Voodoo founded 2013 with co-founder Laurent Ritter (high school friends)
- First product: Quiz-Up quiz game (€280k crowdfunding, 300k players) — failed
- Second product: arcade game — also failed
- Company facing potential bankruptcy after 2-3 years of traditional development

### Decision
Rebuild the entire game development process from scratch using lean startup methodology. Instead of spending months on one game, produce one prototype per developer per week.

### Reasoning
- Traditional game dev = high investment, low predictability
- Lean startup = low investment per experiment, fast validation
- Key insight: **market validation before product investment**

### Outcome
- Led to Paper.io (2016) — first breakout hit
- Established the factory model that would define Voodoo
- Proved that data-driven rapid testing could replace intuition-driven development

### Significance
**This is the founding decision of the Voodoo methodology.** Everything else flows from this moment: CPI testing, kill-fast culture, studio network, scaling playbook.

## 2. Building the Studio Network Model (2016-2018)

### Context
- After Paper.io's success, needed to scale hit production
- Couldn't hire enough internal developers fast enough
- Saw opportunity in the global indie developer community

### Decision
Create a publishing platform where external studios submit prototypes, Voodoo tests them, and scales the winners. Revenue split: Voodoo takes 80-90%, developer gets 10-20%.

### Reasoning
- Indie devs have creativity but lack UA, monetization, and testing infrastructure
- Voodoo has infrastructure but can't generate enough creative ideas internally
- Network model = unlimited creative supply + centralized testing/scaling

### Outcome
- Grew to 700+ studio partners at peak
- ~75% of released titles came from external partners
- Generated 2/3 of revenue from publishing
- Created competitive pressure as rivals offered 30% rev-share

### Significance
Turned Voodoo from a studio into a **platform**. The studio network is the moat — not any individual game.

## 3. Goldman Sachs $200M Investment (May 2018)

### Context
- Hypercasual was exploding; Voodoo needed capital to scale UA and expand
- Multiple investors interested after Paper.io, Helix Jump success

### Decision
Accept Goldman Sachs' $200M investment — the largest French tech fundraising since 2015.

### Priorities Yazdi Set Post-Investment
1. International expansion (offices in Berlin, Montreal, Istanbul, Ukraine)
2. Diversification beyond hypercasual
3. Strategic acquisitions

### Significance
Signaled early awareness that hypercasual alone wasn't a forever business. Yazdi was already planning diversification in 2018, years before the market forced it.

## 4. Lowering KPI Thresholds (c. 2020-2021)

### Context
- Original thresholds (D1 > 55%, D7 > 22%, CPI < $0.25) were missing potentially successful games
- Recognized that rigid metrics created a type of survivorship bias

### Decision
Lower thresholds to D1 > 45%, D7 > 13%, CPI < $0.20. Accept more games into the funnel at the cost of lower average hit rate.

### Reasoning
- Some games with lower initial metrics had long-term potential
- The old thresholds were "short-sighted" — optimized for early performance, not lifetime value
- "KPIs have surprised us over and over again" (Alexander Shea)

### Significance
Shows willingness to evolve the methodology itself. The system tests itself, not just the games.

## 5. The Hybrid Casual Pivot (2022-2023)

### Context
- Apple's IDFA changes (2021) devastated hypercasual economics
- Rising CPIs, declining ad inventory value
- Market saturation: too many copycats, novel concepts impossible to distinguish
- All successful hypercasual games published in 2022 were existing titles, not new ones

### Decision
Declare "hypercasual is dead" and pivot to hybrid casual. Cut studio network from 700 to ~100 teams. Shift from publishing 4+ games/month to ~4 games/year.

### Reasoning
- Hybrid casual: deeper gameplay, multiple monetization loops, longer retention
- Smaller portfolio of $20-100M/year games > large portfolio of rapidly-declining titles
- Organizational restructuring: launch team (new games) + live team (existing games)
- Required fundamentally different capabilities: game economy design, progression systems, live ops

### Outcome
- Mob Control: $200M+ annual run-rate
- Block Jam: ~$100M annual revenue
- €250M hybrid casual revenue in 3 years
- By 2023, hypercasual = only 25% of revenue; by 2024, 22%

### Significance
**The biggest strategic bet of Yazdi's career.** Voluntarily dismantling the business model that made Voodoo famous. Proved the methodology (test-fast, kill-fast, data-driven) could survive a genre transition.

## 6. BeReal Acquisition (June 2024)

### Context
- Voodoo generating €500M+ in gaming revenue, highly profitable
- BeReal: viral photo app, declining users, losing €2.6M/month, not monetized
- Yazdi looking to diversify beyond gaming

### Decision
Acquire BeReal for €500M (cash + earnout).

### Reasoning
- Apply Voodoo's UA expertise and monetization capabilities to a social app
- Social + gaming = Tencent playbook (smaller scale)
- BeReal had brand recognition and authentic product DNA
- Yazdi's thesis: "redefine the future of digital entertainment"

### Outcome (by 2025)
- BeReal now breaking even
- €30M revenue (from zero)
- User base stabilized; growing in Japan
- On path to profitability by 2027

### Significance
Tests whether Voodoo's data-driven methodology extends beyond gaming. If it works, it validates Yazdi as a platform builder, not just a game publisher.

## 7. Decision Pattern Analysis

### Yazdi's Decision Framework (Inferred)
1. **Data first**: Never bet big without metric validation
2. **Fail fast, but fail cheaply**: Keep experiments small until data confirms
3. **Scale what works**: Once data confirms, pour resources aggressively
4. **Kill without sentiment**: Abandon failing approaches regardless of historical investment
5. **Evolve the system**: The methodology itself is subject to testing and iteration
6. **Think ahead**: Begin diversifying before the current model shows decline (2018 Goldman Sachs priorities already included diversification)

### Recurring Pattern
**Explore → Test → Kill/Scale → Saturate → Pivot → Repeat**

This pattern appears at every level:
- Individual game level: prototype → CPI test → kill or scale
- Genre level: hypercasual → saturate → pivot to hybrid casual
- Company level: gaming → saturate → diversify into social apps
