## 1. Mobile Free To Play Interview (Comprehensive Voodoo Deep-Dive)

One of the most detailed public interviews about Voodoo's methodology.

### Key Revelations

**On CPI Testing:**
- Voodoo's original thresholds: D1 retention > 55%, D7 retention > 22%, CPI < $0.25
- Studios can launch free test campaigns from Day 1 via Voodoo's proprietary dashboard
- Results come back "in a matter of days" — game is classified as "future Hit", "high potential", or "kill"
- "It's about the LTV of users, and there's only so much a seven-day retention will capture"

**On Developer Relations:**
- Voodoo works with "all developers they discuss with" — low barrier to entry
- Developers typically need 5-10 prototypes before first hit
- Voodoo seeks "developers that have raw talent and that are hungry for success, regardless of location or the size of the team"
- Publisher handles UA creative testing; studios focus on core development

**On Publishing Volume:**
- Approximately 4 games published monthly (in hypercasual era)
- Revenue split: ~2/3 from publishing third-party, ~1/3 from in-house development

## 2. GameWorldObserver / Games and Names Podcast (2023)

Focus on the hybrid-casual transition.

### Key Metrics Shared
- Hybrid-casual targets: 45% D1, 15% D7 (20% for puzzle), 10% D30
- Definition: "deep core gameplay with at least 15% D7 retention and multiple monetization loops beyond ads"
- Thibault de Vésinne-Larüe (VP): "We're a bit more prescriptive...but we still have the same process of reviewing ideas, testing fast, killing fast, iterating, soft-launching fast"

### Kill/Scale Philosophy
- Speed of killing is a competitive advantage, not a weakness
- Daily playtime tracking added as engagement metric
- Monetization now examines highest-grossing titles within genre for applicable strategies

## 3. PocketGamer.biz Coverage

### "The Voodoo Approach" (Feature Article)

**On Prototyping:**
- Development begins with rapid prototypes in days using basic Unity assets
- "Janky" internal toys test core interactions before full development
- 2-4 weeks to reach releasable quality once concept proves fun
- Full cycles complete in 3-4 months

**Alexander Shea (Head of Publishing) on KPIs:**
> "We've become very flexible with KPIs, because KPIs have surprised us over and over again."
- CPI target typically below 20 cents at launch
- Retention ranges vary significantly by genre

**Simon Hill (Studio Head) on the Fun Factor:**
> "Without that really strong, resonant feeling...you will have trouble with your day one retention."
- Games must activate "system one" thinking — brain's intuitive response requiring no explanation

**"Hypercasual is Dead" (PGC London 2023):**
- Alex Shea declared: "Hypercasual is dead"
- Evidence: All successful hypercasual games Voodoo published in 2022 were existing titles, not new ones
- Market oversaturation made novel concepts nearly impossible to distinguish
- Triggered diversification into casual and midcore publishing

## 4. Deconstructor of Fun Analysis (2024-2025)

### "Voodoo's Secret Sauce: From 0 to 250M Hybridcasual Revenue in 3 Years"
- Organizational shift from 700 studios to ~100 teams
- Three-phase launch: Prototyping → Soft Launch → Full Launch
- ROAS target: 150% at launch to indicate viability
- ~100 prototypes in development continuously, ~1,000 tested annually, ~4 launched, ~1 hit
- **Conversion rate: 0.4% from prototype to commercial hit**

### "Why Most Games Deserve to Die" (2025)
- Prototype volume doubled to ~2,000/year
- Thibault de Vésinne-Larüe: "An extra iteration is a lost opportunity"
- True cost = opportunity cost, not development hours
- Games advance only with "exceptional potential" signals
- Scaling: core teams grow from 5 to 50+ members
- ROAS targets: 150% at D120, 200-300% over time

### Gabriel Rivaud (VP Gaming):
> "The best studios are made up of people whose life mission is to make an iconic game."

## 5. Bloomberg / TechCrunch / Sifted (Business Press)

- €500M acquisition of BeReal (cash + earnout)

## 6. Substack Deep-Dive (Alexandre.substack.com)

Third-party analysis by Alexandre (not Yazdi) provided detailed business model breakdown:

**On Early Failures:**
- First product Quiz-Up: 300k players at launch via €280k crowdfunding, ultimately failed
- Subsequent arcade game also failed
- These failures drove the pivot to lean methodology

**On Revenue Sharing:**
- Voodoo's typical split: 10-20% to developer
- Competitors started offering 30% to poach studios
- This competitive pressure on rev-share contributed to network evolution

**On Goldman Sachs Investment (2018, $200M):**
- Yazdi outlined three priorities: international expansion, diversification beyond hypercasual, strategic acquisitions
- Led to offices in Berlin, Montreal, Istanbul, Turkey, Ukraine
