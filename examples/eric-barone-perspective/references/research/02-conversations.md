# Agent 2：长对话与即兴思考

## 调研范围
Reddit AMA、GDC相关报道、独立游戏媒体采访、播客

## 一手来源

### 1. Game Developer（原Gamasutra）采访 — 开发周期
> "I did make a few minor projects prior to Stardew Valley (mostly unfinished), but I always worked alone and tried to figure things out from scratch."
> "I never really participated in any game jams or anything, I just set a huge goal and then adopting a mindset of never giving up until it's created."
> "I told myself 'Why not me?' I adopted a mindset of tenacity and optimism."
> "And when it came to pixel art or other things that I had little experience in, I just dove in and did my best. As time went on I started to get better at these things through hundreds of hours of practice."
> "I would've felt ashamed if I had gotten so far and then quit. I wanted to see my idea become reality."
> "I've always been obsessed with creating things and seeing them through to completion, and I also really wanted to become successful on my own terms."

来源：https://www.gamedeveloper.com/production/how-i-stardew-valley-i-creator-eric-barone-coped-with-a-four-year-dev-cycle

### 2. Game Developer — 自我施加的Crunch
> "On average, I probably worked on it 10 hours a day every day of the week during development."
> "You should be free to work yourself to the bone, but not to force someone else to do that for you."
> "The development was characterized by phases of insane productivity followed by phases where I hardly worked at all."
> "It does help if you can absolutely convince yourself that you're destined for greatness. It's not even an ego thing--it's just a way to prevent doubt and insecurity from hindering you."
> "It's both passion and discipline. I think it's a lot easier to stay driven when you're doing your own project, knowing that there are no limits to how far you can go."
> "There were times during development that I didn't feel like working, that I even wanted to quit entirely."
> "My strategy with the community is simple: no strategy at all! I think that, as an indie developer, you should just be yourself and be a real human."

来源：https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/the-4-years-of-self-imposed-crunch-that-went-into-i-stardew-valley-i-

### 3. Aftermath采访 — 无法放手Stardew Valley
> "There's certain aspects to Stardew Valley now that are inextricable from the community that's formed around it."
> "I feel responsible for any issues that people have, or if anyone doesn't enjoy the game or has a bad time."
> "Once I reopen the book on Stardew, I always have a hard time closing it again because I always want to add more things, make it better."
> "Every single thing I'm adding, I'm thinking about how people are going to play this and talk about it and love it. It's gonna be part of their experience."
> "It would probably be difficult, and it would probably inevitably mean that I would not focus on Haunted Chocolatier because I would feel like I want to weigh in on and contribute to stuff."
> "I don't feel like I owe anyone anything when it comes to Haunted Chocolatier. It's a game that I'm deciding to make. I don't need to make it."
> "My goal in life isn't about making money. I want to create things and share them with the world. That's what it's all about."

来源：https://aftermath.site/stardew-valley-concernedape-haunted-chocolatier-interview/

### 4. Nintendo Life采访 — 为什么不能放弃
> "I feel like I could work on it for the rest of my life, and just keep improving it, adding more stuff to it."
> "I don't want to see it fizzle out, I want to keep it alive."
> "It's a rare opportunity I have that there's so many people who love this game and will play whatever update I make."
> "What I love to do is create things and then share them with people and give people experiences."
> "With the creative stuff, I'm very particular and I want to make sure that it's my vision."
> "Make it work, make it fun for the player—that's my angle."
> "Somehow, even though it's gotten very popular, still kind of has this really small indie feel to it."

来源：https://www.nintendolife.com/features/i-donrt-want-to-see-it-fizzle-outr-stardew-valley-creator-on-why-he-cant-give-it-up

### 5. PlayStation LifeStyle采访 — 创意焦虑
> "I think it makes sense that I worked entirely alone. I wanted to do all the music, the art."
> "I didn't really have any sort of deliberate plan or anything. I just had my intuition as to what was the next important thing I should work on."
> "It was definitely a struggle to keep my sanity."
> "I just persevered and forced myself to learn. You realize the thing that you thought was good actually isn't. You realize why and you improve on it. And that's just an endless cycle."
> "In fact, I thought it was bad." （关于对Stardew Valley的自我评价）
> "I had to prove that I wasn't just crazy, that this wasn't just a pipe dream, and that I was actually gonna follow through on it. That was really getting to me, psychologically, thinking about whether I was wasting my life, wasting my time. So part of my strategy was to convince the people around me that they should believe in me. But that was difficult. I was definitely embarrassed."

来源：https://www.playstationlifestyle.net/2018/11/09/stardew-valley-creator-eric-barone-will-help-smash-your-creative-anxiety/

### 6. PC Gamer GDC 2025采访 — 专注Haunted Chocolatier

> "I have this problem where once I start, I can't stop."

来源：PC Gamer GDC 2025报道

## 即兴思考特征
1. **极度坦诚的自我暴露**：公开说过想放弃、觉得自己的作品差、心理挣扎
2. **没有"方法论"的直觉驱动**：反复说"no deliberate plan"、靠直觉判断优先级
3. **矛盾的自我认知**：一方面说"convince yourself you're destined for greatness"，另一方面说"I was definitely embarrassed"
4. **对"完成"有执念**：ashamed if quit、obsessed with completion、can't stop once started
