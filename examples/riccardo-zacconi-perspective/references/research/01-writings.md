# Riccardo Zacconi - Writings & Published Content Research

> Research date: 2026-04-12
> Sources: Public interviews, podcast transcripts, conference presentations, IPO filings

---

## 1. Overview

Riccardo Zacconi is notably low-profile compared to other gaming industry CEOs. He has no personal blog, no regular column, and minimal social media presence. His intellectual output exists primarily in:
- Long-form podcast interviews (Secret Leaders, Made IT, EU-Startups)
- Conference appearances (Slush 2023, various gaming conferences)
- Investor-facing materials (King IPO S-1 filing, investor presentations)
- Occasional press interviews (CNBC, European CEO, VentureBeat, GamesIndustry.biz)

## 2. Key Long-Form Interview Sources

### 2.1 Secret Leaders Podcast (Episode 38, ~2019)
- Discusses giving King his all: selling car, flat, living on friend's couch for 2.5 years
- Angel investment arriving at the "eleventh hour" (Christmas Eve 2003 fax machine story)
- "University of Life" philosophy over formal education
- "King is harder to get into than Harvard" — hiring standards

### 2.2 Made IT Podcast (Episode 112, Masterclass Edition)
Most detailed articulation of his frameworks:
- **Chessboard marketing strategy**: Start with $100, analyze cohort, double if metrics justify
- **Retention as the supreme KPI**: "The most important key KPI is retention — is the user coming back?"
- **Day-1 retention threshold**: 40% = good, 65% = exceptional (Candy Crush achieved 65%)
- **3-month payback period** on marketing spend enabling rapid scaling
- **Freemium virality engineering**: 5-lives system = pay $0.99 OR invite 3 friends
- "I believe in profitable companies. It's the only way to sustain growth."
- Split team strategy during Facebook pivot: 50% maintain revenue, 50% experiment

### 2.3 EU-Startups Podcast (Episode 2, 2020)
- Early career path: LEK consulting → BCG → Spray → uDate → King
- Importance of working at successful companies before founding
- European founder mindset gaps: language, global thinking, ambition

### 2.4 Slush 2023 (with Sofia Dolfe, Index Ventures)
- "Innovation involves taking proven elements and combining them in a distinctive way"
- Day-one retention as primary success metric
- AI's potential in performance marketing and adaptive gameplay
- Mobile games as "crown jewel of European tech"

### 2.5 Index Ventures Profile
- Mordillo cartoon analogy for strategic awareness: things look good close up, danger visible only when zoomed out
- "The most important thing of all was to not tell beautiful lies"
- Bubble Saga as "the turning point" (March 2011)

## 3. IPO & Investor Materials

### King Digital Entertainment IPO (March 2014, NYSE)
- Filed September 2013, priced March 26, 2014 at $22.50/share
- Company valued at $7.08 billion — largest mobile/social gaming IPO in US history
- Key narrative: King is NOT a one-hit wonder; portfolio of 180+ game IPs
- Methodical data-driven approach to game development
- Revenue growth story: EUR 45.8M (2011) → EUR 1.39B (2013)

### Activision Blizzard Acquisition (2016)
- $5.9 billion acquisition price
- Bobby Kotick cited Zacconi's leadership and executive team as decisive factor

## 4. Key Quotes Compilation

**On risk:**
- "I always say choose the path of the unknown. Whether you're an entrepreneur or not, the biggest risk is to not take risks."
- "That's the difference between an entrepreneur and an investor. An investor tries to hedge their bets, but an entrepreneur can't. You have to go all in."

**On hiring and team:**
- "For me, it's all about bringing on board people who are better than me and letting them do what they're best at."
- "When you trust people and you treat them like adults, amazing things can happen."
- "It's more difficult to get into King than to get admitted into Harvard."

**On honesty:**
- "The most important thing of all was to not tell beautiful lies."
- "Treat others like you want to be treated — no bullshit."

**On product:**
- "I believe in profitable companies. It's the only way to sustain growth."
- "The most important key KPI is retention — is the user coming back?"

**On innovation:**
- "Innovation involves taking proven elements and combining them in a distinctive way."
- "I came up with 50 ideas. And one of these ideas was to do online dating. I killed the other 49."

**On leaving King:**
- "Continue hiring people that are better than you are (and let them do), continue treating others how you want to be treated and continue the relentless quest to provide the most fun to our players."
