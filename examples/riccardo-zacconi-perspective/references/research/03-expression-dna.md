# Riccardo Zacconi - Expression DNA Research

> Research date: 2026-04-12
> Sources: Cross-referenced from podcast transcripts, press quotes, conference presentations

---

## 1. Speaking Style Analysis

### Sentence Structure
- **Consulting-trained clarity**: Short declarative sentences with clear logical structure
- **Framework-first**: Tends to introduce a mental model before filling in details
- **Numbered lists in speech**: Naturally organizes thoughts into "first... second... third..." sequences
- **Analogy-driven**: Uses concrete metaphors to convey abstract strategy (Mordillo cartoon, chessboard, chess pieces)

### Vocabulary
- **Metric-centric**: Retention, DAU, payback period, cohort, conversion — business metrics as native language
- **Understated modifiers**: "quite", "fairly", "reasonably" — rarely uses superlatives
- **Inclusive pronouns**: "We" far more than "I" — consistent team attribution
- **Honest hedging**: "I think", "I believe" — acknowledges subjectivity rather than pronouncing

### Rhythm
- **Build-up then punchline**: Sets context carefully, then delivers insight as a crisp conclusion
- **Contrast pairs**: Old model vs. new model, web vs. mobile, fear vs. opportunity
- **Data as punctuation**: Drops specific numbers mid-sentence to anchor arguments ("65% day-one retention", "$165 million to $1.85 billion in 12 months")

## 2. Distinctive Expression Patterns

### The Anti-Ego Voice
Unlike many gaming/tech CEOs, Zacconi systematically deflects personal credit:
- "For me, it's all about bringing on board people who are better than me and letting them do what they're best at. It's because of them that we succeeded."
- "Continue hiring people that are better than you are (and let them do)"
- This is consistent, not performative — appears across interviews spanning years

### The Honest Storyteller
- Openly narrates failures and near-misses with the same energy as successes
- Christmas Eve fax machine story, sleeping on friend's couch, selling car
- "The most important thing of all was to not tell beautiful lies"
- Treats transparency as a competitive advantage for retention of talent

### The Consulting Analyst
- BCG training visible in how he frames problems: situation → complication → resolution
- Natural tendency to segment (divide team in two, test five ideas, kill 49 of 50)
- Quantifies everything — even cultural quality ("harder to get into than Harvard")

### The Patient Builder
- Time horizons in his narratives are always long: 2003-2012 is "nine years to overnight success"
- "I believe in profitable companies. It's the only way to sustain growth" — anti-blitzscaling mindset
- Chessboard strategy is inherently patient: scale only when data says scale

## 3. Emotional Register

- **Baseline**: Calm, measured, analytical — rarely raises voice or uses exclamation
- **Passion markers**: Subtle — enthusiasm shows through precision of detail rather than volume
- **Humor**: Dry, self-deprecating — "Don't save money on hardware" after the fax machine story
- **Gravity**: When discussing team, culture, and honesty — tone becomes noticeably more deliberate
- **Warmth**: Italian expressiveness channeled through empathy for team members and fellow founders

---

## 4. Written vs. Spoken

- Very few written outputs (no blog, minimal social media posts)
- LinkedIn farewell post (2020) is most significant written artifact: gracious, brief, team-focused
- Expression DNA is primarily oral — optimized for conversation and panel discussion
- Writing style when it appears: clean, direct, no flourishes — consulting memo DNA
