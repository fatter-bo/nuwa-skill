## 2. Criticism and Skepticism

### "One-Hit Wonder" Narrative
- Wall Street analysts persistently questioned whether King was a one-hit wonder
- IPO priced at $22.50, dropped 15% on first day — investor skepticism about franchise durability
- Stock declined ~30% post-IPO before Activision acquisition at $18/share
- Market never fully believed in portfolio diversification story during public period

### Trademark Controversy (2014)
- King filed trademark for "Candy" and "Saga" in video games context
- Filed opposition against Stoic Studios' "The Banner Saga"
- Intense public backlash from gaming community
- Seen as aggressive overreach by a dominant player
- Settled with Stoic in April 2014

### Pac-Avoid Cloning Controversy
- Developer Matthew Cox accused King of cloning his game ScamperGhost as Pac-Avoid
- King admitted fault, took down the game, Zacconi personally apologized
- "King should never have published Pac-Avoid"
- Damaged credibility on IP claims while simultaneously pursuing trademark protection

### Predatory Design Accusations
- Free-to-play model criticized as psychologically manipulative
- "Difficulty spikes" designed to trigger impulse purchases
- Lives system creates artificial scarcity and social pressure
- Some regulators and researchers classify as "dark patterns"
- King's defense: game is fully playable without spending; 70%+ players never pay

### IPO Timing Criticism
- Some analysts viewed IPO timing as opportunistic — cashing out at peak
- Stock price trajectory ($22.50 → ~$15 → $18 acquisition) validated skeptics
- Early investors who sold before Candy Crush explosion missed upside

## 3. Team and Employee Perspectives

### Positive
- Long tenure of co-founders (all stayed through acquisition): unusual in gaming
- "When you trust people and treat them like adults" — reflected in Glassdoor reviews
- Strong engineering culture in Stockholm office
- Data science team given real influence over game design decisions

### Mixed
- Rapid scaling (110 → 2,000 in ~2 years) inevitably strained culture
- Some tension between data-driven optimization and creative freedom
- Barcelona, Berlin, and US offices had different sub-cultures
- Post-Activision integration period caused departures

## 4. Industry Context: How Peers View King's Model

### Respect for Data Approach
- King's data-driven level design became industry benchmark
- "Retention always wins" widely adopted as industry principle
- A/B testing of difficulty curves now standard practice

### Skepticism About Replicability
- King's success seen as partially lucky timing (mobile transition 2012)
- Match-3 genre dominated before others caught up
- Supercell's approach (small teams, kill fast) seen as alternative model
- Dream Games (Royal Match) proved match-3 not winner-take-all

### European Gaming Ecosystem
- King proved European studios could build global consumer hits
- Inspired generation of Stockholm/London/Berlin game studios
- Sweet Capital now funds next generation of European gaming companies
