### 1.1 Gamasutra/Game Developer — "A Personal Journey: Jenova Chen's Goals for Games"
- 最全面的长篇采访之一
- 核心引用：
  - "We build our games like a Japanese garden, where the design is perfect when you cannot remove anything else."
  - "For adults to enjoy something, they need to have intellectual stimulation, something that's related to real life."
  - "Games are not good enough for adults."
  - "Can games make you and another human experience an emotion that's deep enough to touch adults?"

### 1.3 Vice — "Jenova Chen Is Still Trying to Convince the World Video Games Are Art"
- 核心引用：
  - "When I was in school, I really saw myself as a solo artist. I'm trying to prove this can be fine art."
  - "I want to put this game in the gallery and in the museums."
  - "That's such an insult to an artist. Who cares about money?"
  - 反思人生剩余约2,030个周日，以此为动力
  - "Am I going to stay focused on first trying to be a fine artist, or should I change my approach and do it in a way that could actually convince more people that games are art?"
- URL: https://www.vice.com/en/article/jenova-chen-is-still-trying-to-convince-the-world-video-games-are-art/

### 1.4 FamousAspect — "What is Game Design with Jenova Chen"
- 核心引用：
  - "Game design is interaction design. It's about using the interactive design, the input and output to generate emotional response in the user or the audience."
  - "I really don't like game design. I like game creation or game direction."
  - "The game industry today has very limited emotional courage."
  - "There's no reference of sadness. There's no reference of sorrow, no reference of sublime."
  - "Each game I do is actually an experiment to verify a theory."
  - "I always seek for theories and knowledge from the established medium...stand on the shoulder of giants."
- URL: https://famousaspect.com/what-is-game-design-with-jenova-chen/

## 4. 高频引用句整理

> "We build our games like a Japanese garden, where the design is perfect when you cannot remove anything else."

> "Game design is interaction design — it's about using the interactive design, the input and output to generate emotional response."

> "Each game I do is actually an experiment to verify a theory."

> "Nobody is born toxic."

> "The game industry today has very limited emotional courage."

> "If you look at genres of films, they're all divided by emotions. But when you look at video games, what feeling do you get? It's usually a feeling of accomplishment."

> "I want the interaction to be about exchange of emotions or feelings, rather than exchange of blows."

> "Games are not good enough for adults."

> "I always seek for theories and knowledge from the established medium...stand on the shoulder of giants."
