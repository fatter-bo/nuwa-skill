## 1. Key Interviews & Longform Content

### 1.1 The Block Interview (Token 2049, September 2022)

Larsen's most significant post-collapse interview. At Token 2049 in Singapore, he addressed critics head-on:

- On criticism of P2E exploitation: "It's mind-boggling to me. It's unfair, but I guess that's just the way it is when you're getting the attention of the world."
- On the economy: "Once we get the game to be more developed, I have faith that the economy will also stabilize."
- On the hack: "Obviously, looking back at it, I wished we would have focused more on security. It was a hard lesson to learn."
- Acknowledged: "There was a lot of money flowing into the ecosystem, and the underlying kind of the product or the game quality was not high enough to sustain that level of speculation."

Source: https://www.theblock.co/post/174933/axie-infinity-bounce-back-founder-interview-leonard-larsen

- How Sky Mavis approaches boom/bust cycles "with a grain of salt"

### 1.3 Token 2049 Dubai Keynote (May 2025)

Larsen's most mature public reflection on the Web3 gaming space:

- "People have put money into developers building AAA-style games that take years to ship. There's too little crypto innovation and too much focus on graphics over gameplay and economy."
- "$15 billion" directed toward "the wrong developers" funding projects that merely raise capital rather than innovate
- "The bright side of this is that the bad actors are being washed out" - Darwinian market view
- Argued games must choose between graphics, gameplay, and economy - can only excel at two
- Sky Mavis chose gameplay + economy over graphics

Source: https://www.blockchaingamer.biz/news/38788/sky-mavis-aleksander-larsen-web3-gaming-failures-token2049/

- "Pain has a tendency to either break things or bring people closer together." - on the Ronin hack
- "We're opening up Ronin to more builders. With DeFi, meme coins, and new gaming experiences, we're entering a new phase where community-driven innovation will thrive."
- Emphasis on sustainable tokenomics and organic user engagement over short-term speculation

## 2. Official Axie Infinity / Sky Mavis Communications

### 2.1 SLP Monetary Policy Update (February 2022)

Official blog post acknowledging economic crisis:
- "The Axie economy requires drastic and decisive action now or we risk total and permanent economic collapse"
- Reduced SLP emissions from adventure mode and daily quests to zero (cutting daily supply by 56%)
- Introduced 2% deflation target mechanism

Source: https://blog.axieinfinity.com/p/slpcap

### 2.2 Axie Infinity Whitepaper - Design Principles

Three founding principles:
1. Gamers should be rewarded for the value they create in games
2. Gamers should be able to trade their game assets with anyone, anywhere
3. Games should be owned by the communities that play them

Source: https://whitepaper.axieinfinity.com/team

## 3. Departure Announcement (February 2026)

Aleksander Larsen announced his departure from Sky Mavis after nearly 8 years, effective February 11, 2026.

Two reasons cited:
1. Family commitments - three-month-old daughter, cannot spend 100+ days annually in Vietnam
2. Security concerns from the hack experience: "a stark reminder that some adversaries are more sophisticated, persistent, and well-funded than others. Not all competition is friendly."

Next move: defense industry in Europe, amid rising geopolitical threats from Russia.

Source: https://www.blockchaingamer.biz/news/23598/jobs-news/

## Key Recurring Themes Across All Writings

1. **Gamer-first identity**: Always frames himself as a gamer who happens to build blockchain products
2. **Honest about failures**: Consistently acknowledges the hack and economic collapse as learning moments
3. **Gameplay > Graphics**: Repeated insistence that crypto games should prioritize economy and gameplay
4. **Long-term conviction**: Despite setbacks, maintains belief in blockchain gaming's potential
5. **Community ownership**: Core philosophical commitment to players owning their digital assets
6. **Pragmatic optimism**: Acknowledges problems while maintaining forward-looking stance
