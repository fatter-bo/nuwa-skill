## 1. Critical Perspectives

### 1.1 TIME Magazine - "Axie Infinity Has Left Filipino Gamers Despondent + In Debt" (July 2022)

The most damaging mainstream media coverage:
- Scholars owing hundreds/thousands of dollars to friends/family
- Monsters became worthless after economic collapse
- SLP crashed from $0.40 to $0.02 (95% decline)
- Filipino players who quit day jobs for Axie left with nothing
- Implicit comparison to predatory economic structures

Source: https://time.com/6199385/axie-infinity-crypto-game-philippines-debt/

### 1.2 Rest of World - "To Infinity and Back" (2022)

Deep investigative piece:
- Sky Mavis didn't notice $620M hack for 6 days
- YGG's ~30,000 Filipino scholars earning approximately $20/month post-collapse
- DAU dropped from 2.7M to 1.5M before the hack even happened
- Structural analysis: economy relied entirely on new player inflows
- "Not all of the $600M stolen was players' money" - but the optics were devastating

Source: https://restofworld.org/2022/axie-infinity-hack/

### 1.3 Ponzi Scheme Accusations

Multiple critics compared the P2E model to a Ponzi scheme:
- New player money paid existing players
- When new player growth slowed, the economy collapsed
- Sky Mavis disputed: "Focusing on growing a network through early incentives does not make a Ponzi scheme."
- Academic analysis: resembled a Ponzi in practice even if not by design

### 1.4 Exploitation Narrative

Key criticism points:
- Players in developing countries worked for sub-minimum-wage returns
- Scholarship system created hierarchical labor extraction
- Guild managers (often foreign) profited from Filipino labor
- Players took on debt to buy Axies that became worthless
- Sky Mavis benefited from marketplace fees while players lost money

### 1.5 Deconstructor of Fun - Economic Analysis

Professional game design analysis:
- Identified "death spiral" risk in tokenomics before collapse
- SLP inflation structurally unsustainable
- Breeding economy required infinite growth
- No meaningful sink for tokens beyond breeding more Axies
- "The game economy was a ticking time bomb"

Source: https://www.deconstructoroffun.com/blog/axie-infinity-deconstruction

## 5. The Ronin Hack - External Security Analysis

### 5.1 FBI Attribution
- Linked to North Korea's Lazarus Group and APT38
- Sophisticated spear phishing of a single Sky Mavis employee
- Attackers compromised 4 of 9 validators on Ronin's proof-of-stake system
- $620M stolen (largest crypto hack in history at the time)

### 5.2 Security Community Assessment
- Validator set too small and too centralized
- Bridge security was a known weak point in the industry
- 6-day detection delay was "unacceptable" per security experts
- Post-hack security improvements (more validators, better monitoring) were necessary but reactive

### 5.3 US Government Response
- Treasury Department sanctioned Lazarus Group wallet
- FBI confirmed North Korean attribution
- Approximately $30M recovered by law enforcement
- Highlighted state-sponsored cyber threats to crypto infrastructure
