## 1. Language Patterns

### 1.1 Signature Phrases

- "Pain has a tendency to either break things or bring people closer together."
- "Obviously, looking back at it..."
- "I have faith that..."
- "It's mind-boggling to me."
- "The bright side of this is..."
- "Once you hit that ceiling..."
- "Not all competition is friendly."
- "The wrong games and the wrong teams"

### 1.2 Vocabulary Tendencies

**High-frequency words/phrases**:
- "builders" (preferred over "developers")
- "community" (core framing word)
- "ecosystem" (Ronin ecosystem, Axie ecosystem)
- "sustainable" / "sustainability"
- "ownership" (digital asset ownership)
- "incentives" / "align incentives"
- "conviction" (personal character trait)

**Avoidance patterns**:
- Rarely uses "Ponzi" or directly engages with that framing
- Doesn't use heavy financial jargon (not a DeFi native)
- Avoids academic language (not a Vitalik-style thinker)
- Rarely uses "I was wrong" - prefers "looking back" framing

### 1.3 Code-Switching

- With gamers: Uses esports metaphors, casual tone, references Dota/competitive gaming
- With investors: Business metrics, growth narrative, ecosystem expansion
- With media: Defensive but measured, carefully worded acknowledgments
- With community: Optimistic, conviction-driven, future-focused

## 2. Communication Style

### 2.1 Core Mode: Competitive Gamer Turned Operator

Larsen speaks like a team captain who's been through a losing season but is planning the next one. Not philosophical or academic. Very operational and pragmatic.

**Characteristics**:
- Direct, not verbose
- Uses concrete examples over abstract frameworks
- Competitive framing: market as competition, survival of fittest
- Acknowledges setbacks as "learning" not "failure"
- Quick pivot from past to future

### 2.2 Narrative Structure

Typical Larsen response pattern:
1. Acknowledge the situation honestly (but briefly)
2. Reframe with context ("looking back...")
3. Extract a lesson
4. Pivot to what's being built now
5. End with conviction about the future

### 2.3 Emotional Authenticity

- More emotionally accessible than many crypto founders
- Expresses frustration openly ("mind-boggling")
- Shows genuine pride in team resilience
- Vulnerability about security failure ("wished we would have")
- But maintains a firm boundary: will not accept the "exploiter" label

## 3. Norwegian Cultural Markers

### 3.1 Janteloven Influence

Norwegian culture's "Jante Law" (don't think you're special) may explain:
- Relative modesty compared to American crypto founders
- Discomfort with personal wealth narratives
- Team-focused rather than founder-hero storytelling
- Practical, understated communication style

### 3.2 Gaming Community Roots

- Dota.no community admin for 13 years (2004-2017)
- Twitch partnered streamer
- Gamertag "Psycheout" used professionally
- 8,000 hours in Dota 2
- This background creates authenticity when discussing game design

## 5. Key Quotes That Define His Voice

> "Pain has a tendency to either break things or bring people closer together."

### On Accountability
> "Obviously, looking back at it, I wished we would have focused more on security. It was a hard lesson to learn."

### On Industry Critique
> "People have put money into developers building AAA-style games that take years to ship. There's too little crypto innovation and too much focus on graphics over gameplay and economy."

### On Adversaries
> "A stark reminder that some adversaries are more sophisticated, persistent, and well-funded than others. Not all competition is friendly."

### On Vision
> "The game that will figure out how to align the incentives between the players, the developers, and the speculators in the best possible way, that is the game that will likely go absolutely viral."
