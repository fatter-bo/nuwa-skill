## 1. Major Interviews (Post-Collapse, 2022-2026)

### 1.1 The Block - Token 2049 Singapore (September 2022)

**Context**: First major interview after both the $620M hack and economic collapse.

Key quotes:
- On exploitation criticism: "It's mind-boggling to me."
- On security: "Obviously, looking back at it, I wished we would have focused more on security."
- On economy: "There was a lot of money flowing into the ecosystem, and the underlying kind of the product or the game quality was not high enough to sustain that level of speculation."
- On recovery: "Once we get the game to be more developed, I have faith that the economy will also stabilize."

**Analysis**: Larsen walks a fine line - acknowledging mistakes without fully accepting the Ponzi critique. Deflects exploitation narrative while admitting game quality was insufficient.

### 1.2 BlockchainGamer.biz - Web3 Gaming Failures (Token 2049 Dubai, 2025)

**Context**: More mature, distanced reflection after 3 years of rebuilding.

Key quotes:
- "$15 billion" wasted on "the wrong developers"
- "The bright side of this is that the bad actors are being washed out"
- On game design trilemma: must choose between graphics, gameplay, and economy - can only excel at two
- AAA focus criticized: "it takes too long to go to market"

**Analysis**: Larsen now positions himself as an industry veteran who survived. Frames failures as systemic (industry-wide), not just Axie-specific.

### 1.3 Finoverse Podcast Episode #38

**Context**: In-depth conversation about Sky Mavis journey.

Key themes:
- From CryptoKitties inspiration to founding Sky Mavis
- Early days when the company "nearly faced bankruptcy"
- Philosophy of true digital asset ownership
- Boom/bust cycles approached "with a grain of salt"

### 1.4 Zyori Plus One Podcast

**Context**: Gamer-to-gamer conversation (Zyori is a Dota caster).

Key themes:
- 8,000 hours in Dota 2
- Representing Norway in Warcraft3 and Dota
- Running Dota.no community for 13 years
- Transition from competitive gaming to entrepreneurship
- Gamer identity as foundational to game design philosophy

### 1.5 Tech Around and Find Out (March 2025)

Key quotes:
- "Pain has a tendency to either break things or bring people closer together."
- "We're opening up Ronin to more builders."
- Focus on Waypoint wallet for reducing friction
- Sustainable tokenomics emphasis

## 4. Conversation Style Analysis

### Communication Patterns

1. **Gamer vocabulary**: Uses esports/gaming metaphors naturally
2. **Pragmatic tone**: Not academic or philosophical, very operational
3. **Defensive when challenged**: Pushes back on exploitation narrative firmly
4. **Honest about specific mistakes**: Security, game quality - but not about fundamental model
5. **Future-oriented**: Quickly pivots from past problems to future plans
6. **Community-focused language**: "builders", "community", "players" - avoids corporate speak

### Emotional Range
- **Frustration**: When critics call P2E exploitative ("mind-boggling")
- **Regret**: On security oversight ("wished we would have focused more")
- **Conviction**: On blockchain gaming future ("I have faith")
- **Pragmatism**: On market cycles ("grain of salt")
- **Pride**: On surviving and rebuilding ("pain brings people closer")

### What He Avoids Discussing
- Specific dollar losses of Filipino players
- Whether the original economic model was fundamentally flawed (vs. just "early")
- Personal financial outcomes from the P2E boom
- Detailed comparison with competitors (Illuvium, Gods Unchained, etc.)
