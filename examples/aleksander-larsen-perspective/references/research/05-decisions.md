# Aleksander Larsen - Key Decisions & Turning Points Research

> Research date: 2026-04-12
> Sources: interviews, media coverage, on-chain data, company announcements

---

## 1. Founding Decision: From CryptoKitties to Axie Infinity (2018)

**Context**: Larsen was playing CryptoKitties and recognized the potential of blockchain-based digital asset ownership in games.

**Decision**: Co-found Sky Mavis in April 2018, based in Vietnam, to build a blockchain game combining Pokemon-style creature collecting with competitive battles.

**Reasoning**: 
- True digital ownership could transform gaming
- Games should reward players for value creation
- Vietnam offered strong engineering talent at lower cost

**Outcome**: Created what became the most successful blockchain game in history (by peak users).

---

## 2. Building Ronin Sidechain (2020-2021)

**Context**: Ethereum gas fees made Axie transactions prohibitively expensive for the target audience (often earning <$10/day).

**Decision**: Build a custom Ethereum sidechain (Ronin) with only 9 validators for speed and low cost.

**Reasoning**:
- Players needed free/cheap transactions
- Ethereum L1 was too expensive and slow
- Centralized validator set enabled speed

**Outcome**: 
- Enabled massive user growth (to 2.7M DAU)
- BUT: centralized validator set became the attack vector for the $620M hack
- Classic speed-vs-security tradeoff that haunted the team

**Lesson**: "Once you hit that ceiling... it's difficult to get back to equilibrium."

---

## 3. The SLP Economic Model

**Context**: Needed a way to reward players while funding game development.

**Decision**: Dual-token model with AXS (governance) and SLP (in-game earnings). SLP could be earned through gameplay and spent on breeding new Axies.

**Reasoning**:
- Players earn SLP through daily play
- SLP spent on breeding creates demand
- AXS for governance and staking
- Marketplace fees fund development

**Outcome**:
- Worked brilliantly during growth phase (SLP at $0.40, players earning $2,000/month)
- Death spiral when growth slowed: SLP inflation exceeded demand
- Required emergency action: cut emissions by 56% in February 2022
- Official statement: "The Axie economy requires drastic and decisive action now or we risk total and permanent economic collapse"

## 4. Response to the $620M Hack (March 2022)

**Context**: March 23, 2022 - North Korean hackers stole $620M via compromised validators. Sky Mavis didn't discover the theft until 6 days later.

**Decision chain**:
1. Freeze Ronin Bridge immediately upon discovery (March 29)
2. Coordinate with FBI and law enforcement
3. Raise $150M from Binance (April 2022) to reimburse victims
4. Commit to full reimbursement of all user funds
5. Increase validator count and improve security monitoring
6. Relaunch Ronin Bridge with enhanced security

**Key Larsen statement**: "This was a social engineering attack combined with human error from December 2021. Sky Mavis tech is solid."

**Analysis**: The reimbursement commitment was crucial for survival. Many crypto projects collapse after major hacks. The Binance-led fundraise was a vote of confidence. But the 6-day detection delay and the "tech is solid" claim drew criticism given the centralized validator architecture was a design choice.

## 5. Pivot from "Play-to-Earn" to "Play-and-Earn" (2022-2023)

**Context**: The P2E economic model collapsed. New narrative needed.

**Decision**: 
- Rebrand from "play-to-earn" to "play-and-earn"
- Launch Axie Origins with free-to-play access
- Introduce seasonal runes/charms system (items expire after 6-8 week seasons)
- Create SLP sinks beyond breeding
- Focus on gameplay quality over earning potential

**Reasoning**:
- "Play-to-earn" attracted speculators, not gamers
- Game quality must justify the economy
- Seasonal resets create recurring engagement and spending
- Free-to-play removes the "investment" framing that led to debt

**Outcome**: Smaller but more sustainable player base. The promise of "fun first, earn second" became the new narrative.

---

## 6. Workforce Reduction (November 2024)

**Context**: Post-boom adjustment, need to focus resources.

**Decision**: Cut 21% of workforce despite claiming "year of valuable growth."

**Focus areas after cuts**:
- Ronin Wallet and Waypoint
- Mavis Marketplace
- Axie Infinity (Origins + upcoming Atia's Legacy)
- Web3 game publishing
- Ronin Network expansion

**Reasoning**: "Not due to financial concerns" but strategic realignment for 2025+. Focus on core strengths rather than trying to build for all users.

---

## 7. Atia's Legacy MMO Development (2024-2026)

**Context**: Need a new flagship game to revitalize the Axie brand.

**Decision**: Develop Axie Infinity: Atia's Legacy, a full MMO with:
- Squad-based combat with four Axies
- Story-driven missions
- Resource and crafting mechanics
- Social town hub
- "Risk-it-all" extraction dungeons (lose items on death)
- Deep integration with existing Axie NFTs and land

**Status**: Playtest 1 ran September-October 2025 (1,000 players). Playtest 2 launched April 2026.

## 8. Personal Decision: Leaving Sky Mavis (February 2026)

**Context**: After nearly 8 years, Larsen chose to leave the company he co-founded.

**Reasons stated**:
1. Family: three-month-old daughter, cannot spend 100+ days/year in Vietnam
2. Security experience: The Ronin hack as "a stark reminder that some adversaries are more sophisticated, persistent, and well-funded than others"

**Next move**: Defense industry in Europe, citing rising geopolitical threats from Russia.

**Analysis**: This is a remarkable career pivot. The man who built the biggest blockchain game is moving to defense, explicitly motivated by the experience of being attacked by a nation-state (North Korea). The Ronin hack didn't just cost $620M - it fundamentally changed Larsen's worldview about what kind of adversaries exist.

## Decision Pattern Analysis

### Recurring Themes:
1. **Speed over safety (then correcting)**: Ronin's 9 validators, then expanding post-hack
2. **Growth optimism**: Assumed growth would solve economic problems
3. **Honest but late acknowledgment**: Admits mistakes but usually after the damage
4. **Community commitment**: Consistent on reimbursement and rebuilding
5. **Pragmatic pivots**: Willing to change narrative and strategy when needed
6. **Competitive instinct**: Frames every challenge as a competition to win

### What He Got Right:
- Identifying blockchain gaming potential early
- Building in Vietnam (talent + cost advantages)
- Raising emergency capital after the hack
- Committing to user reimbursement
- Pivoting to free-to-play

### What He Got Wrong:
- Underestimating security risks (centralized validators)
- Overestimating economic model sustainability
- Not building adequate token sinks before growth
- 6-day hack detection delay
- Not warning players about financial risks of "earning" model
